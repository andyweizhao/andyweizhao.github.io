----------------------- REVIEW 1 ---------------------
SUBMISSION: 77
TITLE: X-lingual Metric: Machine Translation Evaluation without Human References
AUTHORS: Wei Zhao, Yang Gao and Steffen Eger

----------- Contributions, main strengths and weaknesses -----------
The paper proposes a novel metric called MoverScore, which combines contextualized word embeddings with Word Mover Distance to improve the correlation between the metric scores with the actual human assessments.

Strengths:
1. Interesting idea that is based on a well-defined content of Earth Mover Distance
2. The novel metric is extended to try to not use any parallel data by exploiting cross-language embeddings
3. The paper provides an analysis of possible reasons of some of the non-positive results they got.

Weaknesses:
1. The varian using cross-language embeddings are not performing at the same level as the one using parallel data
----------- Overall recommendation -----------
SCORE: 4 (Strong: I would like to see it accepted.)
----------- Justification -----------
The paper shows a nice piece of research that totally fits the topics of EurNLP. The results look promising and the technology developed and researched will generate lots of discussions at the conference.



----------------------- REVIEW 2 ---------------------
SUBMISSION: 77
TITLE: X-lingual Metric: Machine Translation Evaluation without Human References
AUTHORS: Wei Zhao, Yang Gao and Steffen Eger

----------- Contributions, main strengths and weaknesses -----------
This abstract describes an ongoing follow-up work to the work on MoverScore, a contextualized-word-embedding based evaluation method for machine translation.
In this work, the authors try to extend the approach to use cross-lingual word and sentence representations, towards reference-free machine translation evaluation.
The initial results are inconclusive, where cross-lingual word embeddings work better than cross-lingual sentence representations, but not better than BLEU.
I find this research direction interesting and novel, and as it clearly challenges the common status-quo on MT evaluation I think it suits the EurNLP program.
----------- Overall recommendation -----------
SCORE: 4 (Strong: I would like to see it accepted.)
----------- Justification -----------
The approach is creative and novel: even though the results are inconclusive or negative, I think this is an interesting work in progress.
